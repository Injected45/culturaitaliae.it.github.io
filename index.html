The place of culture

We are very grateful to Prof. Stefano Manzocchi and Prof. Pier Luigi Sacco for hosting in the first issue of 2024 of the prestigious Confindustria Economic Policy Review dedicated to Culture an article, written with the young cultyst Walter Simonis, on the experience of Cultura Italiae as a promoter of the relationship between Culture and Economy. The place of culture. Industry, well-being, development. Our country is a place of culture. But what is the place of culture in our society and economy today? The reason for this issue of the Journal of Economic Policy is that the field of cultural services and heritage largely expresses the identity of the society in which it is expressed and in which it carries out its activities, as well as contributing to the tangible and intangible well-being of the people involved in it as providers or users of cultural services. Without claiming to be exhaustive, we therefore present in this volume a survey of the structure and recent evolution of the cultural sector in our country, as well as an incomplete examination of important aspects related to the production and use of cultural services.

International Conference on Industrial Tourism

Cultura Italiae and Turismo Italiae are very pleased to invite you on Friday 1st and Saturday 2nd December in Ivrea in the Spaces of Officina H, in Via Monte Navale, 2, for the second edition of the International Conference of Industrial Tourism Organized by Federturismo Confindustria, Confindustria Canavese, Confindustria Canavese – Tourism, Culture and Sport Group, in collaboration with Confindustria Piemonte, Unione Industriali Torino and Associazione Archivio Storico Olivetti, with numerous prestigious patronages, including ENIT – National Tourism Agency, Piedmont Region, City of Ivrea, Confindustria Emilia Area Centro and Unione Industriale Biellese, CulturaItaliae and Turismo Italiae with the contribution of TIM, Olivetti, Turin Chamber of Commerce and Spritz. After the success of the first edition, we have doubled the appointment, including two half-days and four panels, to offer a unique opportunity to explore the different facets of industrial tourism, with training sessions, prestigious keynote speakers and guided tours of iconic industrial places in the cities of Ivrea, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Biella and Turin.
